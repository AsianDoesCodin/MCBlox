What makes McBlox different from Modrinth and CurseForge?

McBlox isn't a modpack launcher — it's a game launcher.

Modrinth and CurseForge are built for vanilla+ modpacks that extend Minecraft's core loop: more blocks, more mobs, more progression. McBlox was built for something else entirely.

McBlox was born from the CustomNPCs community, where creators build full games inside Minecraft — RPGs, story-driven adventures, puzzle maps, minigames, and experiences where the gameplay is defined by the creator, not by mining and crafting. These games exist, but most players never find them because there's never been a place to discover and play them. Now there is.

Open McBlox. Browse. Pick a game. Click Play. That's it.

No modpack setup. No manual installs. No configuring Java. McBlox handles everything — downloads, mod loaders, libraries, assets — all behind the scenes. You set your keybinds, graphics, FOV, and sensitivity once, and they carry across every game.

McBlox is where you go to play, not to configure.

RPG. Minigame. Story. Puzzle. The limit is at the hands of the creators.
